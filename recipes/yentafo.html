Yen Ta Fo Recipe

Description

Yen Ta Fo is a traditional Thai noodle soup characterized by its pink broth, which is made from fermented red tofu. It typically includes rice noodles, a variety of seafood or meat, and different vegetables. This dish is popular as a street food in Thailand and is known for its unique flavor profile, combining spicy and funky elements.

Ingredients

  • Broth: Made from fermented red bean curd (Tao Hu Yee) or a specially flavored pink sauce, which gives the soup its signature pink color and umami flavor. 2
  • Noodles: Typically served with wide rice noodles, thin rice noodles, or vermicelli.
  • Toppings: Commonly includes fish balls, fried tofu, squid, morning glory, and crispy wontons. 2
  • Sauce: The pink sauce is essential and can be made with red fermented tofu, birdseye chillies, Thai sriracha sauce, tomato ketchup, garlic, vinegar, and pickled garlic. 2
  • Seasoning: Adds sweetness, tanginess, and umami, often including garlic, sugar, and vinegar. 2 Yen Ta Fo is a popular street food in Thailand, enjoyed for its unique flavors and colorful presentation.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Pink Sauce: Blend red bean curd, pickled garlic, fresh garlic, chilies, sriracha, tomato paste, vinegar, sugar, and fish sauce until smooth. Simmer gently for 5–10 minutes to meld flavors. Set aside.
  2. Make the Broth: In a pot, combine stock, coriander roots, daikon, soy sauce, sugar, white pepper, and salt. Simmer 15–20 minutes, then strain. Keep warm.
  3. Cook Proteins: Blanch fish balls, tofu puffs, and seafood in boiling water until cooked. Blanch water spinach for 20–30 seconds until bright green. Drain and keep warm.
  4. Prepare Noodles: Soak or boil rice noodles according to package instructions until tender but firm. Drain and portion into serving bowls.
  5. Fry Garnishes: Fry garlic in oil until golden for garlic oil. Deep-fry wonton wrappers until crisp. Drain and set aside.
  6. Assemble Bowls: Add 2–3 tbsp pink sauce to each bowl. Place noodles, then top with fish balls, tofu, seafood, and water spinach. Ladle hot broth over the ingredients. Drizzle with garlic oil, sprinkle fried garlic, and add crispy wontons. Garnish with cilantro, green onions, lime wedges, and optional chilies. Serve immediately. Tips & Variations
